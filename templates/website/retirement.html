How does retirement plan work?

A pension plan is a retirement plan that requires an employer to make contributions into a pool of funds set aside for a worker's future benefit.

The pool of funds is invested on the employee's behalf, and the earnings on the investments generate income to the worker upon retirement.

In the simplest sense, retirement planning is the planning one does to be prepared for life after paid work ends, not just financially but in all aspects of life. The non-financial aspects include lifestyle choices such as how to spend time in retirement, where to live, when to completely quit working, etc. A holistic approach to retirement planning considers all these areas.

The emphasis one puts on retirement planning changes throughout different life stages. Early in a person's working life, retirement planning is about setting aside enough money for retirement. During the middle of your career, it might also include setting specific income or asset targets and taking the steps to achieve them.
